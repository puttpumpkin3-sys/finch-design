Annotation · brand mark
The 36×36 teal tile uses the same Newsreader italic "cc" as the favicon — they're explicitly the same mark at different scales. Tile uses linear-gradient(180deg, --teal, --teal-deep) with an 8px radius and a 2px rgba(13,111,122,0.4) shadow. The "cc" glyph is Newsreader 22 italic with -0.04em letter-spacing — tight enough that the two c's lock together visually.
Annotation · why split layout
Two reasons over a single centered card. (1) The right pane substitutes for a marketing site — first-time visitors see voice and intent before signing in. (2) The teal mass anchors the page so the form can stay genuinely small and parchment-warm without floating in white space. Below 980px the right pane hides entirely (single column, scrolls — see Section 04 mobile).
Annotation · Google button order
Google sits above the email/password form. Per founding-cohort feedback the majority sign in via Google. Email is the fallback. The mono "or" divider is intentionally spare — not a giant horizontal rule.
Annotation · invite-only language
"Founding cohort is invite-only. Need access?" — italic Newsreader, single line, centered under the submit. The link opens a contact form at /access; no public sign-up route exists. This keeps the cohort intentional and prevents random sign-ups from hitting Supabase.

Annotation · why "cc" not a logo
A typographic monogram survives at 16×16 where a custom logo glyph won't. The italic "cc" is also already in use on the rail brand mark in the app shell — this just re-uses it. Decision: do not commission a custom logo for V1. Once the app finds product-market fit, revisit.
Annotation · 16px legibility
At 16×16, the double "cc" becomes mush. The 16px PNG drops to a single italic "c" at 85% of the tile height — the same letterform, just isolated. The SVG keeps the full "cc" because vector handles sub-pixel rendering better. ICO bundles 16/32/48 so legacy IE / Chromium pickers grab the right size.

Login implementation lives in src/app/auth/. Use existing Supabase Auth client (@supabase/ssr) — wrap browser-only access in useMemo with typeof window === 'undefined' guard per CLAUDE.md §11. Drop favicon files into public/.
